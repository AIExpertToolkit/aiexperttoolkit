A Simple Way to Start 60-minute setup

Pick your lane

  • Product pages (Shopify/Amazon), or
  • Blog + SEO packages, or
  • Ad copy + landing page refreshes

Set your basics

  • Create a one-page voice guide: 5 example lines to match.
  • Save 2–3 go-to prompts (grab ours below).
  • Build a basic intake form: specs, audience, claims to avoid, competitors.
  • Make three samples in your lane and post them (simple site or doc).

That’s enough to pitch and deliver real work.

Three Plug-and-Play Workflows

1) Product Pages (PDPs) — great for e-commerce

  • Inputs: SKU, features, materials, dimensions, who it’s for, brand voice lines, main keyword.
  • Generate in Writesonic: title (≤120 chars), 5 benefit-led bullets, 120–200-word description, 3 FAQs, alt text.
  • Polish: read aloud, cut fluff, verify facts, avoid risky claims.
  • Deliver: Google Doc or CSV for upload.

Mini Prompt:

“Write a product title (≤120 chars), 5 benefit-led bullets, a 150-word description, and 3 FAQs. Voice: [5 sample lines]. Audience: [persona]. Primary keyword: [kw]. Include these specs: [list]. Avoid any health/medical claims. Return bullets as a simple list.”

2) Blog + SEO Posts — great for lead gen & authority

  • Inputs: topic, target keyword, 3 competitor links, voice lines.
  • Generate: outline → draft → meta title/description.
  • Polish: add examples, internal links, and a strong intro/CTA.
  • Deliver: Google Doc; offer 1 revision.

Mini Prompt:

“Create an outline, then a 1,200-word post on [topic] for [audience]. Tone: [brand voice]. Primary keyword: [kw]. Add a short intro hook, subheads, a quick ‘how-to’ section, and a concise CTA. Suggest 2 internal links.”

3) Ad Copy Variants — great for testing

  • Inputs: product, angle (pain/proof/price/luxury/social), voice lines.
  • Generate: 10 headlines + 5 primary texts.
  • Polish: remove jargon; keep to platform limits.
  • Deliver: Sheet with columns for variant, angle, character count, link.

Mini Prompt:

“Generate 10 headlines (≤40 chars) and 5 primary texts (≤125 chars) for [product]. Angles: pain, proof, price, luxury, social. Match this voice: [5 lines]. Keep it clear and specific.”

Quick QA Checklist (use every time)

  • Accuracy: specs and claims correct?
  • Clarity: short sentences, clear benefits first.
  • Voice: matches the 5 lines you were given.
  • SEO touch: main keyword in title + first sentence; natural placement elsewhere.
  • Format: headings, bullets, FAQ present and tidy.

Common Pitfalls (and easy fixes)

  • Over-promising: no medical/financial claims. Keep benefits believable.
  • Wall-of-text: break into bullets and short paragraphs.
  • No voice guide: always ask for 3–5 lines to copy the tone.
  • Skipping a second read: read it out loud once; you’ll catch 80% of issues.

How to Land Your First Clients (without ads)
